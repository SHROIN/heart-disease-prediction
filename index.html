Lower your daily sodium intake

It reflets your heart has to work harder to move the additional fluid through your body. Choose foods labeled as “no salt added,” try to avoid foods that have more than 400 milligrams of sodium per serving, and try to stay below 1500 milligrams total per day.

Choose heart-healthy fats

Unsaturated fats can be heart-healthy by lowering inflammation in your body. Inflammation can lead to heart disease. Heart-healthy fats include vegetable oil, low fat mayonnaise, and oil-based salad dressings.

Eat lots of fruits and vegetables

Fruits and veggies are packed with nutrients and are lower in calories to help you maintain a moderate weight and reduce inflammation. The more colorful and fresh your choices, the better.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the common risk factors for heart disease and how can we handle them?

Smoking, high blood pressure, diabetes, and obesity are common contributors to heart disease.

How can lifestyle choices, such as diet and exercise, impact heart health?

Adopting a healthy diet, engaging in regular exercise, and managing stress can significantly improve heart health.

What are the warning signs and symptoms of a heart attack and how does this happen?

Chest pain, shortness of breath, and discomfort in the upper body are warning signs that may indicate a heart attack.

How is high blood pressure related to heart disease, and what steps can be taken to manage it?

Hypertension is a major risk factor; lifestyle changes and medication can help manage blood pressure levels.

Are there genetic factors that contribute to the risk of developing heart disease?

Family history can influence heart disease risk, and understanding genetic predispositions is crucial for preventive measures.

What role does cholesterol play in heart health, and how can it be managed?

Monitoring cholesterol levels through a balanced diet and medication can reduce the risk of heart disease.

Can stress and mental health impact the risk of heart disease, and if so, what are effective coping strategies?

Chronic stress can contribute to heart problems; adopting stress-reducing activities and seeking mental health support are important.

What are the different types of heart diseases, and how do their symptoms and treatments vary?

Conditions like coronary artery disease, heart failure, and arrhythmias have distinct symptoms and treatments, requiring specialized care.
